  1. Question

    In the following figure the distributions of a variable given by two samples (A and B) are represented by parallel boxplots. Which of the following statements are correct? (Comment: The statements are either about correct or clearly wrong.)


    1. The location of both distributions is about the same.
    2. Both distributions contain no outliers.
    3. The spread in sample A is clearly bigger than in B.
    4. The skewness of both samples is similar.
    5. Distribution A is left-skewed.

    Solution

    1. False. Distribution B has on average higher values than distribution A.
    2. True. Both distributions have no observations which deviate more than 1.5 times the interquartile range from the box.
    3. True. The interquartile range in sample A is clearly bigger than in B.
    4. True. The skewness of both distributions is similar, both are about symmetric.
    5. False. Distribution A is about symmetric.

    Two Sample t-test

data:  Waiting by Supermarket
t = -2.9847, df = 117, p-value = 0.003456
alternative hypothesis: true difference in means between group Sparag and group Consumo is not equal to 0
95 percent confidence interval:
 -2.7480469 -0.5558352
sample estimates:
 mean in group Sparag mean in group Consumo 
             4.103493              5.755434

    Which of the following statements are correct? (Significance level 5%)


    1. The absolute value of the test statistic is larger than 1.96.
    2. A one-sided alternative was tested.
    3. The p-value is larger than 0.05.
    4. The test shows that the waiting time is longer at Sparag than at Consumo.
    5. The test shows that the waiting time is shorter at Sparag than at Consumo.

  3. Question

    A survey with 50 persons was conducted to analyze the design of an advertising campaign. Each respondent was asked to evaluate the overall impression of the advertisement on an eleven-point scale from 0 (bad) to 10 (good). The evaluations are summarized separately with respect to type of occupation of the respondents in the following figure.

    To analyze the influence of occupation on the evaluation of the advertisement an analysis of variance was performed:

      Res.Df    RSS Df Sum of Sq    F   Pr(>F)
1     49 23.350                           
2     46 20.315  3     3.035 2.29 0.090773

    Which of the following statements are correct?


    1. The fraction of explained variance is smaller than 1414%.
    2. A one-sided alternative was tested for the mean values.
    3. The test statistic is larger than 19.919.9.
    4. The fraction of explained variance is larger than 5555%.
    5. It can be shown that the evaluation of the respondents depends on their occupation. (Significance level 55%)

    Solution

    In order to be able to answer the questions the fraction of explained variance has to be determined. The residual sum of squares when using only a single overall mean value (𝑅𝑆𝑆0\mathit{RSS}_0) as well as the residual sum of squares when allowing different mean values given occupation (𝑅𝑆𝑆1\mathit{RSS}_1) are required. Both are given in the RSS column of the ANOVA table. The fraction of explained variance is given by 1−𝑅𝑆𝑆1/𝑅𝑆𝑆0=1−20.315/23.35=0.131 - \mathit{RSS}_1/\mathit{RSS}_0 = 1 - 20.315/23.35 = 0.13.

    The statements above can now be evaluated as right or wrong.


    1. True. The fraction of explained variance is 0.1300.130 and hence smaller than 0.14.
    2. False. An ANOVA always tests the null hypothesis, that all mean values are equal against the alternative hypothesis that they are different.
    3. False. The test statistic is F=2.290F = 2.290 and hence not larger than 19.919.9.
    4. False. The fraction of explained variance is 0.1300.130 and hence not larger than 0.55.
    5. False. The pp value is 0.09080.0908 and hence_not_ significant. It can not be shown that the evaluations differ with respect to the occupation of the respondents.

  5. Question

    In a small city the satisfaction with the local public transportation is evaluated. One question of interest is whether inhabitants of the city are more satisfied with public transportation compared to those living in the suburbs.

    A survey with 250 respondents gave the following contingency table:

               Location
Evaluation  City Suburbs
  Very good   29      25
  Good        37      31
  Bad         27      56
  Very bad     7      38

    The following table of percentages was constructed:

               Location
Evaluation  City    Suburbs
  Very good    11.6    10.0
  Good         14.8    12.4
  Bad          10.8    22.4
  Very bad      2.8    15.2

    Which of the following statements are correct?


    1. The percentage table provides total percentages.
    2. The percentage table can be easily constructed from the original contingency table: percentages are calculated for each row.
    3. The value in row 3 and column 1 in the percentage table indicates: 10.8 percent of the respondents lived in the city and evaluated the public transportation as bad.
    4. The value in row 1 and column 2 in the percentage table indicates: 10 percent of those living in the suburbs evaluated the public transportation as very good.
    5. The percentage table provides the location distribution for each level of satisfaction.

    Solution

    In the percentage table, the total sums are about 100 (except for possible rounding errors). Hence, the table provides total percentages, i.e., the relative frequencies for each combination of location type and satisfaction level.


    1. True. The total sums are about equal to 100 (except for possible rounding errors).
    2. False. This calculation yields row percentages. But the table provides total percentages.
    3. True. This is the correct interpretation for total percentages.
    4. False. This is an interpretation for column percentages, but the table provides total percentages.
    5. False. The row sums are not equal to 100.
